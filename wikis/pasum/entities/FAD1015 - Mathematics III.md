# FAD1015 - Mathematics III

PASUM Foundation course covering **Statistics and Probability Theory** with applications in R programming and matrix algebra.

## Course Overview

This course introduces students to fundamental concepts in probability theory, statistical distributions, hypothesis testing, and matrix operations.

## Topics Covered

### 1. Probability Fundamentals (Weeks 1-3)
- Counting Rules & Permutations
- Mutually Exclusive Events & Conditional Probability
- Independent Events & Bayes' Theorem

### 2. Random Variables (Weeks 4-6)
- Discrete Random Variables (PDF & CDF)
- Mean & Variance (Discrete & Continuous)
- Continuous Random Variables

### 3. Probability Distributions
- [[Binomial Distribution]] — discrete, fixed trials
- [[Poisson Distribution]] — discrete, rate-based
- [[Normal Distribution]] — continuous, bell curve
- [[Uniform Distribution]] — continuous, equal probability
- [[Exponential Distribution]] — continuous, memoryless

### 4. Statistical Inference
- Sampling Distribution of the Mean
- Estimation of Population Mean
- [[Hypothesis Testing]] About the Mean
- Hypothesis Testing in R

### 5. R Programming
- Input Data & Descriptive Statistics in R
- Sampling Methods
- Statistical Computations

### 6. Linear Algebra
- [[Matrices]] — Types & Operations
- Matrices — Inverse & Systems of Equations
